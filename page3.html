#EverGreen

                                        

A movement where everyone is invited.

"Earth does not belong to us: we belong to the Earth"
- Marlee Martin

As Marlee Martin stated, the Earth does not belong to us: we belong to the Earth.

It is not new to us, people, that Earth is the only planet that can sustain life so far. According to religous passages, it took God six days to create this beautiful Earth, but then it took us decades to destroy it and people still managed to scratch the Earth's surface. 

 Floods became fatal to living things, air became gray, diseases grew in numbers and many other signs that our beloved Earth is slowly fading away.

When I was still a kid, everybody loves the rain. We used to play around our place when it's raining, but now along with fun there is fear. Fear of having flood where water might be as high as the roof because of poor waste management.

Drainage system are clogged with garbages from undisciplined people. Deforestation is also a factor in controlling floods. Because people are merciless in cutting trees, soil became denser than before resulting inability to absorb water.

Deforestated mountains and those that have been industrialized have resulted to landslides.

The ozone layer is already damaged because of the machineries and inventions that are made for the convenience of the people. This might also result to unbearble heat and climate change that we, people, are now experiencing.

The planet is slowly getting hotter and hotter. If we continue to destroy earth, it will burn us as well. 

Air pollution is now evident, specially in the cities. Smoke coming from cars, burning of wastes, cigarettes, etc. mix with air that is continually roaming around the earth.

The wrong works of man comes back to all of us.

A famous line says "change begins with you". Change doesn't happen overnight but if we work in unity, we can save this beautiful Earth. All the words that comes out in your mouth will not be counted if you don't put it in actions. 

All the words that comes out in your mouth will not be counted if you don't put it in actions. 

Now that we are aware of what is the real scenario in the planet we live in, we should look back to what we have been doing in our daily lives.

Do I put my trash  inside the proper garbage bin?

When I have a food wrapper that I'm finished with and there's no bins present around me, did I keep it with me first or did I just throw it away anywhere?

Do I try to lessen the use of single use plastics or the disposable ones when there's a chance?

Your answers to these simple questions will reflect on your attitude towards our surrounding. These simple acts, if not applied in our lifestyle, will definitely cause a great effect on the cummulative number of trash improperly disposed.

Waste management is very important in making our earth cleaner. And the most important value in life that we need to achieve the proper waste management is discipline. 

The most important value in life that we need to achieve the proper waste management is discipline. 


Everyone's participaton is a must. A single human being can't  save the earth, but a community can.
